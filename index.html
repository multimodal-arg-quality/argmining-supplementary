Do you see what I mean? Assessing Multimodal Argumentation Quality in Scientific Charts

This companion site presents the complete corpus of 50 argumentative visualizations analyzed in our paper, with quality scores grounded in the L1–L4 semantic content hierarchy, and sample generated claims. All charts are drawn from IPCC assessment reports, national climate assessments, and energy technology reviews across two scientific domains.

Key Definitions

TermDefinition
L1–L4Four-level semantic content model (after Lundgard & Satyanarayan, 2021): L1 = chart structure, L2 = statistical patterns, L3 = perceptual salience, L4 = semantic meaning.
AlignClaim–evidence alignment: do L2 patterns directly depict the L4 claim? (1–5)
WStrWarrant strength: is the L1→L2→L3→L4 reasoning chain coherent and recoverable? (1–5)
SRelSalience–relevance match: do L3 salient features serve the L4 argument? (1–5)
QComposite quality: Q = (Align + WStr + SRel) / 3
TiersTop (Q ≥ 4.0), Mid (3.0 ≤ Q < 4.0), Bottom (Q < 3.0)
Semantic ConvergenceThe degree to which a reader recovers the argument the designer intended, measured across claim (SC1), evidence (SC2), and warrant (SC3) levels.
