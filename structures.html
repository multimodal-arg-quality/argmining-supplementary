Charts by Argument Structure

Charts organized by their Multimodal Argument Graph (MAG) template. Each template describes the structural relationship between claims and evidence.

Jump to: SC-SE · SC-CE · SC-CompE · MC-ME

SC-SE: Single Claim – Single Evidence

The simplest argumentative structure. One claim is supported by one piece of visual evidence through a single warrant. These charts typically make a focused, specific point.

SC-CE: Single Claim – Contrastive Evidence

A single claim is supported by evidence that relies on contrast or comparison between categories, time periods, or conditions. The warrant involves comparing visual elements to establish the claim.

SC-CompE: Single Claim – Complementary Evidence

A single claim is supported by multiple complementary pieces of evidence that together build the argument. Often involves multi-panel charts where different panels contribute different aspects of the evidence.

MC-ME: Multiple Claims – Multiple Evidence

Complex argumentative structures where multiple claims are supported by multiple evidence streams. These charts encode rich, layered arguments often involving causal chains or synthesis across data dimensions.
